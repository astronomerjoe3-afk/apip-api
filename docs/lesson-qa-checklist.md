# Lesson QA Checklist

Use this checklist before approving any F4+ lesson.

## Concept and Teaching

- [ ] The central law, relationship, or reasoning rule is stated explicitly.
- [ ] The lesson explains what the formula means, not just how to substitute numbers.
- [ ] The lesson names the main misconception tags it is repairing.
- [ ] The lesson includes at least one common trap or non-example.
- [ ] The analogy includes mapping and a stated limit.

## Representation and Visuals

- [ ] The lesson uses words, formula, and at least one of diagram, graph, table, or model.
- [ ] The core visual includes the key relationship or comparison.
- [ ] Labels, captions, and formulas fit on desktop and mobile cards.
- [ ] If the topic is graph-based, there is at least one graph-reading task.

## Simulation and Reflection

- [ ] The learner can manipulate a real variable in the explorer.
- [ ] The simulation includes a baseline case and compare-two-cases instruction.
- [ ] The reflection asks for explanation, not recap only.
- [ ] The learner is asked what changed, what stayed linked, and why.

## Assessment

- [ ] Diagnostics have enough variants for fresh attempts.
- [ ] Concept-gate retries rotate to new prompts.
- [ ] Mastery does not reuse diagnostic prompts.
- [ ] Mastery covers multiple subskills without repeated stems.
- [ ] Units and vector direction rules are explicit where relevant.

## Release Gate

- [ ] Nothing in mastery appears for the first time in feedback.
- [ ] No generic fallback copy appears in the live lesson path.
- [ ] Visual and wording QA passed.
- [ ] The lesson passes validate_nextgen_module() after its module package is assembled.
